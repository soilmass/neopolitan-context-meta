## Research caveat

Agency websites in this tier are **notoriously light** on documented "process pages" — many are intentionally vibe-led portfolios. The richest verbatim methodology surfaces in: (a) case-study writeups, (b) journalist interviews (Comm Arts, LBB Online, Codrops), (c) Awwwards conference talk descriptions, (d) studio blogs/Medium accounts.

**Agencies with thin or no verbatim process documentation**: Resn (only "Ideation, Design, and Development" tagline), Mortimer London (site unreachable), Tundra (domain parked), Hardhat NZ (refused fetch), Toy Fight (no methodology), Studio Output (no formal phases), Field.io (no methodology), Mathematic (no process), Antinomy (403 / Awwwards bio only), Edenspiekermann (values-only).

**Agencies with rich verbatim documentation**: Active Theory, Lusion, Hello Monday, Locomotive, Pentagram, Instrument, Work & Co, BASIC/DEPT, Bonhomme, Build in Amsterdam, MakeReign, DEPT, Ueno (cited as a peer reference for the tier).

## Per-Agency Verbatim Findings

### Active Theory (Venice, CA)
Sources: [activetheory.net](https://activetheory.net/), [Comm Arts profile](https://www.commarts.com/features/active-theory), [Andy Thelander Awwwards interview](https://www.awwwards.com/andy-thelander-from-active-theory-designing-with-code.html), [Sundance case study](https://medium.com/active-theory/sundance-new-frontier-3448cf67d2c0)

- **Self-description**: "Creative Technology & Experience Studio" specializing in "building digital experiences through a deep understanding of technical constraints"; blends "story, art & technology as an in-house team of passionate makers"
- **Service taxonomy**: "Creative HTML5 & JS Development, Digital Advertising, Technology Direction, Front & Back-end Development, Art Direction & Design, Creative Direction & Concepting, Digital Installations, and Experiential Campaigns"
- **Methodology — "Designing with Code"** (named methodology): "letting the technology of the project heavily influence the visual style through prototyping and overlapping the design and development phases"
- **Iteration principle**: "Iteration is the essence of the project. Don't be afraid to iterate by small steps"
- **Speed as method**: "Our speed for designing and developing has always been our secret weapon … you can win work with prototypes"
- **Designer/dev parity**: "1:1 ratio of developers to designers"; "We wanted to empower designers by giving them the tools to do more with less code"
- **Distinctive phase — "Polish"**: On their own v6 site: *"two months from start to finish, with polish taking about 80 percent"* — an unusually heavy weighting toward polish
- **Project structure**: Sundance case study identifies sequential phases: **Ideation → Visual Direction → Room Definition → Avatar Development → Technical Implementation**, where "rooms" become the architectural primitive
- **R&D structure**: split portfolio into "client projects and lab experiments"
- **Vocabulary**: "microverses" (vs microsites), "rooms," "polish," "designing with code"

### Lusion (Bristol)
Sources: [lusion.co](https://lusion.co/), [lusion.co/about](https://lusion.co/about/), [Codrops feature 2026](https://tympanus.net/codrops/2026/04/13/lusion-where-digital-craft-meets-ambitious-experimentation/), [Lusion Medium "From Concept Prototyping to Production"](https://medium.com/lusion-ltd/from-concept-prototyping-to-production-in-a-creative-studio-f2083e96c4b9)

- **Three-phase production methodology** (verbatim, from their own Medium):
  1. **Mood Board and Visual References**: *"producing a mood board consisting of the product's patterns/colors/history etc."* to *"define what visual style we are working towards"*; followed by *"a list of visual references that serve as our inspiration … images and video links of other artist's work"*
  2. **Concept Prototyping**: *"Once concepts are mapped out, the prototyping can commence. Here we essentially lay out the foundations of what we will be creating."* Tools: *"rapid prototyping tools, for example, through Houdini FX and Cinema 4D"* to *"quickly lock down the dynamic and visual properties of the product."*
  3. **Production**: *"reproduce the visual prototype in real-time based on the required platforms such as Unity, Unreal, vvvv and WebGL"*; client review checkpoint: *"invite our clients to view their product in real-time animation"*
- **Four service pillars**: Strategy ("Digital Experience Strategy, Technology Strategy, Creative Direction, Discovery, Research") | Creative ("Art Direction, UX/UI Design, Motion Design, Interactive Design, Illustration") | Tech ("WebGL Development, Front End Development, Unity/Unreal, Interactive Installations, AR and VR Experiences") | Production ("Procedural Modeling, 3D Asset Creation, 3D Optimization, Animation, 3D Pipeline Development")
- **Internal R&D**: "Lusion Labs" — "monthly experiment series"
- **Anti-template philosophy**: *"the best ideas usually fall apart the moment you force them into a template … every project gets its own system, its own logic, and its own flavour"*

### Hello Monday / DEPT® (NYC, Copenhagen, Aarhus)
Sources: [hellomonday.com](https://www.hellomonday.com), [hellomonday.com/about](https://www.hellomonday.com/about), [hellomonday.com/work/essential](https://www.hellomonday.com/work/essential)

- **Project filter — "the Fs"**: 8 criteria: "Fun, Fame, Forward, Fortune, Fearless (innovation), Future, Freedom, Footprint." Decision rule: *"If a project doesn't have enough Fs, Hello Monday won't do it."*
- **Process ethos**: *"A key ingredient in our creative process is to prototype our ideas."* Prototypes serve two roles: early — communicate ideas within team and to client; late — *"bridge the gap between design and code — enabling us to break away from siloed thinking and ensuring communication between designers and developers is fluid."*
- **Three-fidelity prototype ladder** (from Essential case study):
  - **Low-fidelity**: *"simple paper sketch, moving into a low-fidelity prototype, helped us collaborate with our 3D Animator"*
  - **Medium-fidelity**: explore interaction timing (e.g. discovered scroll-synced product animation *"provided a jagged experience"*)
  - **High-fidelity**: *"As the product renderings reached their final detail level we did high-fidelity prototypes to test how the product finish fit"*
- **Code of Honor (cultural, not process)**: "Be nice / Use our powers for good / Try the truth / Solve the problem / Enjoy the ride / Speak up and listen / Help each other / Team up"
- **Anti-budget creative principle**: creative partners deliberately isolated from budget discussions because *"numbers are creative cancer"*

### Locomotive (Montreal)
Sources: [locomotive.ca/en/agency](https://locomotive.ca/en/agency), [Awwwards Reinventing Locomotive case study](https://www.awwwards.com/case-study-reinventing-locomotive-r.html), [Locomotive x Chivalry Medium](https://medium.com/@LocomotiveMTL/locomotive-x-chivalry-how-we-became-more-than-just-collaborators-a0338dac16fc), [Lovers Magazine Louis Paquet interview](https://spaces.is/loversmagazine/interviews/louis-paquet)

- **Named methodology — "Triforce"**: *"when operations, design and development come together seamlessly to deliver products that look good, work great and deliver results"*
- **Service taxonomy** (two pillars): **Digital** = "Digital strategy, Content strategy, User experience, Copywriting, Art direction, Web design, Web development, Hosting, E-commerce" | **Branding** = "Brand identity, Logo design, Naming, Design system, Campaign, Content creation, Motion design, Photo direction, Video production"
- **Core principle — "build from scratch"**: *"The technologies are reusable but the projects themselves should always start with a clean slate to ensure uniqueness"*
- **Content-first principle**: *"crafting from great original content … building cohesive digital experiences where content and design work together from the start"*
- **Distinctive phase: long rebrand journey** ("day one to day 600" ≈ 20 months, including "rejected concepts and deep reflections")
- **Vocabulary**: "Triforce," "playground," "discovery factor," "rich-content," "drag navigation principle"

### Pentagram (London/NYC, plus digital)
Sources: [pentagram.com](https://www.pentagram.com), [pentagram.com/news/design-thinking-examined](https://www.pentagram.com/news/design-thinking-examined), [Five Steps in Pentagram's Design Process – Medium](https://medium.com/@tyjuleworthy/five-steps-in-pentagrams-design-process-28e3aaada6a7)

- **Five-phase process** (per Pentagram-aligned write-up): **Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test**
- **Position on Design Thinking itself**: Natasha Jen's talk titled "*Design Thinking Is Bullshit*" — Pentagram is willing to publicly contest the standardised methodology
- **System-first principle**: *"a brand system needs to be dynamic, adaptable, and constantly reconfigured for ever-changing needs"*; *"designers must design for unknown circumstances — designing tools for other people to design things"*
- **Project-specific methodology — WGSN**: 5-phase **Observe → Synthesise → Forecast → Focus → Re-evaluate** with two assessment checkpoints
- **Paula Scher principle**: *"Theoretically, you should be able to recognize something without even seeing the logo"*

### Instrument (Portland, OR)
Sources: [instrument.com](https://instrument.com), [Comm Arts profile](https://www.commarts.com/features/instrument), [Metajive interview](https://www.metajive.com/insights/interviews/instrument/), [Instrument's design-systems essay](https://www.designsystems.com/why-we-build-design-systems-an-agencys-perspective/)

- **Anti-process methodology** (verbatim from JD Hooge): *"we don't try to tell our clients that 'this is our process.'"*; *"The team, process, and deliverables are totally based on what needs to be done in that particular case"*
- **Org as method**: multidisciplinary teams ("little boutique agencies") rather than guilds
- **Deliverable philosophy**: *"rather than a one-off solve, we prefer to deliver a complete design system. This gives the client the ingredients and the recipe, rather than a single prepared meal."* Primary goal: "extensibility"
- **Working stance**: *"Move away from the paradigm from constantly working towards the deliverable and start working in a direction of trying to uncover what is right for the final deliverable"*
- **R&D**: "Labs" — "in-house innovation incubator whose members and projects change quarterly"
- **Founding philosophy**: *"The original business plan was to break the agency model"*

### Work & Co (Brooklyn + 7 cities)
Sources: [work.co](https://work.co/), [Wikipedia: Work & Co](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Work_%26_Co)

- **Operational methodology** (verbatim): "small, senior teams including company partners and founders who are hands-on active members"; "Teams are dedicated to one project at a time — eliminating the need for timesheets"; "fully-dedicated teams"; cross-functional across "design, product, engineering, and QA, with active involvement of all stakeholders starting at kickoff"
- **Process method — collaborative prototyping**: *"sharing working prototypes throughout, rather than unveiling finished work"*
- **Client method**: *"asks its clients to participate directly in an iterative process based on rapid prototyping, continuous testing, and fewer large meetings"*
- **Org method**: "single P&L to encourage company-wide collaboration"

### BASIC / DEPT® (formerly BASIC; NYC + Brooklyn)

- **Tagline as method**: "Easy to understand. Impossible to ignore.™" and *"turn cultural value into company value"*
- **Five service domains**: Strategy, Brand Experience, Digital Experience, Content, Technology
- **CX/Commerce/Product capability flow**: "Market Analysis & Business Case Development → Customer Research, Segmentation & Insights → Journey Mapping & Testing → Innovation Sprints & Prototyping → Experience Strategy & Design"
- **Digital-First Branding flow**: "Brand Ecosystem & Roadmap → Business Intelligence → Brand Positioning & Architecture → Brand Messaging → Visual Identity"

- **Triad model**: *"Combining brand strategy, artistic direction, and technology, the studio brings to life immersive and timeless worlds"*
- **Three service categories**: **Branding** (Strategy, Brand platform, Identity, Logotype, Packaging, Interior design) | **Content** (Film, Shooting, Motion, 3D, Illustration, Social) | **Digital** (Eshop, Corporate website, Immersive website, Installation, WebGL, App)

## (A) Common Stages — The Recurring Methodology Spine

The Awwwards-tier methodology spine is **6 stages**, with vocabulary varying by agency:

| # | Spine stage | Verbatim vocabulary used by tier |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Discovery / Strategy** | "Discovery" (Lusion, Locomotive); "Strategy" (BIA, BASIC, Lusion); "Empathize" (Pentagram); "Kickoff" (Ueno); "Market Analysis & Business Case Development" (BASIC); "Stakeholder Interviews" (BIA); "Define" (Pentagram); "Research" (Lusion) |
| 2 | **Concept / Creative Direction** | "Concept" (Lusion, Resn, Dogstudio's "Concept Design"); "Innovative Concepts" (Immersive Garden); "Ideation" (Resn); "Ideate" (Pentagram); "Creative Direction" (Lusion, AT, Bonhomme); "Innovation Sprints" (BASIC); "lore & narrative" (Antinomy) |
| 3 | **Art Direction / Visual Direction** | "Art Direction" (AT, Lusion, Bonhomme [as "artistic direction"], BIA, Immersive Garden, Locomotive, Mathematic, Dogstudio); "Visual Direction" (AT Sundance case study); "Mood Board and Visual References" (Lusion); "Visual Identity" (BASIC) |
| 4 | **Prototyping** | "Concept Prototyping" (Lusion); "Prototype" (Pentagram, Hello Monday, Work & Co); "Innovation Sprints & Prototyping" (BASIC); "Wireframing & prototyping" (BIA); "rapid prototyping" (MakeReign, Work & Co) |
| 5 | **Design + Development (often parallel)** | "Design" (Resn, Lusion, BIA); "Building" (Ueno); "Production" (Lusion); "Development" (Resn, BIA); "Designing with Code" (AT); "Execution & Optimization" (Locomotive Florida-namesake) |
| 6 | **Launch / Wrap-up / Polish / Awards** | "Wrap-up" + "Project launch" + "Follow-up" + "Awards (optional)" (Ueno); "Test" (Pentagram); "Reporting" + ongoing (Locomotive-namesake) |

**Vocabulary equivalence map** (same idea, different words):
- *Discovery* ≡ *Empathize* ≡ *Immersion* ≡ *Kickoff* ≡ *Strategy*
- *Concept* ≡ *Ideation* ≡ *Ideate* ≡ *Creative Direction* ≡ *Innovative Concepts*
- *Art Direction* ≡ *Visual Direction* ≡ *Artistic Direction* (Bonhomme's French-rooted variant) ≡ *Mood Board phase*
- *Prototyping* ≡ *Innovation Sprints* ≡ *Wireframing* (low-fi end)
- *Design + Development* ≡ *Building* ≡ *Production* ≡ *Crafting*
- *Launch* ≡ *Project launch* ≡ *Wrap-up* ≡ *Ship*

## (C) Secret-Sauce Stages — Distinctive Phases

Phases that **2–3 top agencies have but typical web agencies don't**:

1. **Mood Board / Reference Phase** as a *named* deliverable phase, not just an internal artefact
   - **Lusion** (named Phase 1: "Mood Board and Visual References")
   - **Active Theory** (implicit in "Visual Direction" of Sundance case)
   - **Bonhomme** (artistic direction phase implicit — "immersive and timeless worlds")
   - **Why it matters**: enshrines visual exploration as a billable, time-boxed, client-facing phase, not a hidden warmup

2. **Concept Prototyping** (distinct from "design prototype")
   - **Lusion** (Phase 2: uses Houdini FX, Cinema 4D, vvvv — *not* Figma): visual + dynamic + technical concept locked simultaneously
   - **Active Theory** ("Designing with Code" — overlapping design+dev)
   - **Why it matters**: collapses the design→dev handoff before the brief is approved

3. **Polish Phase** (massively over-weighted)
   - **Active Theory** (verbatim on their v6 site: *"two months from start to finish, with polish taking about 80 percent"*) — a stunning ratio (≈48 days of polish vs ≈12 days of building)
   - Used as a quality moat — most agencies can't budget this much polish

4. **Three-Fidelity Prototype Ladder** (not 1, not 2, but 3 distinct fidelities)
   - **Hello Monday**: paper sketch → low-fi prototype → high-fi prototype with final renders
   - **Why it matters**: explicit in-context test for *"Is a visual idea feasible in code?"* at each fidelity

5. **Lab / R&D Stream parallel to client work**
   - **Active Theory** ("client projects and lab experiments")
   - **Instrument** (named "Labs" — "in-house innovation incubator whose members and projects change quarterly")
   - **Lusion** ("Lusion Labs" — "monthly experiment series")
   - **Why it matters**: capability development is institutional, not opportunistic

6. **Awards Phase** as an explicit (optional) project step
   - **Ueno** (verbatim Phase 6: "Awards (optional)")
   - **Locomotive** ("day 600" rebrand journey explicitly framed as award-pursuing)
   - **Why it matters**: this is the Awwwards tier — submitting for SOTD/Honors is a planned phase with deliverables (case-study writeup, screenshots, video, jury narrative)

7. **Project Filter / Brief-Triage Stage** (deciding what work to take)
   - **Hello Monday** ("the Fs" — 8 criteria; "If a project doesn't have enough Fs, Hello Monday won't do it")
   - **DEPT** ("Growth Invention sprint" as engagement model)
   - **Why it matters**: fronts the methodology with creative-fit triage before scoping

8. **Anti-process / Adapt-per-project Phase Zero**
   - **Instrument**: *"The team, process, and deliverables are totally based on what needs to be done in that particular case"*
   - Operationalised as a dedicated configuration step at project start

9. **Long-arc Rebrand / Self-rebrand journey** (multi-month internal refraction)
   - **Locomotive**: documented 600-day rebrand
   - **Active Theory**: v3 → v4 → v5 → v6 site iterations, each multi-month
   - **Why it matters**: the agency uses its own site as the methodology proving ground

10. **"Awards-grade" Polish Ritual** (Reels, microsites, SOTD submissions)
    - **Locomotive's Dynasty project**: *"No clients, no approvals, just pure creative energy"*
    - **Lusion's "My Little Storybook"** monthly experiment
    - **Active Theory's lab projects**

## (E) Vocabulary Glossary — Awwwards-Tier vs Generic Web-Agency Talk

| Awwwards-tier term | Verbatim source(s) | Generic web-agency equivalent | Why the distinction matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Concept** (as a deliverable) | Lusion, Resn, Dogstudio ("Concept Design"), Immersive Garden ("Innovative Concepts") | "Idea" / "kickoff brief" | A concept is a **time-boxed, sign-offable artefact** with its own approval gate |
| **Art Direction** (or **Artistic Direction** — Bonhomme) | AT, Lusion, BIA, Bonhomme, Locomotive, Mathematic, Dogstudio, Immersive Garden | "Visual design" / "look-and-feel" | Art direction is **upstream of UI design**, governs all visual decisions, and has its own owner (Art Director) |
| **Creative Direction** | AT ("Creative Direction & Concepting"), Lusion | "Design lead" | Operates at the **strategy↔craft** interface, decides what the work is about, not how it looks |
| **Mood Board / Reference list** | Lusion (named Phase 1) | "Inspiration folder" | A formal, shared, client-facing artefact — first deliverable in a paid phase |
| **Creative Territory** | (industry-wide; implicit at Bonhomme, Lusion's "feel") | "Style direction" | A defended creative ___zone___ rather than a single direction; survives revisions |
| **Motion Language / Animation Principles** | Hello Monday (animation primitives), Active Theory ("every frame of motion graphics … performant") | "Animations" / "transitions" | A documented system of timing/easing/choreography rules — design-system-grade for motion |
| **Narrative** / **Lore** / **Storytelling** | Antinomy ("lore & narrative"), Locomotive ("storytelling"), Resn ("emotional storytelling"), AT ("blends story, art & technology") | "Copy" / "messaging" | The **interactive thread** through the experience — separate deliverable from copywriting |
| **Narrative Scroll / Scrollytelling** | Industry-wide (Active Theory, Locomotive case studies) | "Long-form page" | A scroll-driven choreography of content reveals — *"as users move down the page, they trigger animations, transitions, and content reveals in a controlled sequence"* |
| **Microverse** | Active Theory ("shifting the concept of a microsite into something new: a microverse") | "Microsite" | Persistent, social, 3D — designed for long sessions ("13 minutes 21 seconds") |
| **Phygital Installation** | Immersive Garden | "In-store experience" | Physical + digital fused, tracked as a single deliverable |
| **WebGL / Real-time** | Active Theory, Lusion, Bonhomme, Immersive Garden | "Animation" / "video" | Designates **runtime-rendered**, interactive, shader-based work |
| **Polish** (as a phase, not a verb) | Active Theory ("polish taking about 80 percent") | "QA" / "tweaks" | A budgeted, intentional phase — not bug-fix scraps |
| **Lab / Labs** | Instrument ("Labs"), Lusion ("Lusion Labs"), Active Theory ("lab experiments") | "Side project" | Named, staffed, rotating **R&D incubator** |
| **Triforce** | Locomotive | "Cross-functional team" | Operations + design + development as **first-class peers**, not as a service triangle |
| **Designing with Code** | Active Theory | "Coded prototype" | Design and dev **simultaneous**, not sequential — a methodology, not a tool |
| **Multi-sensory Design** | Build in Amsterdam | "UX" | Includes sound, motion, haptics as design substrate |
| **Design System** (Instrument's framing) | Instrument ("ingredients and the recipe, rather than a single prepared meal") | "Style guide" | An **extensible kit** that the client can re-cook, not a one-shot deliverable |

## Bottom-line takeaways for the context-site-build skill library

1. **The Awwwards-tier spine is 6 stages, not 5**: Discovery / Concept / Art Direction / Prototyping / Design+Development / Launch+Polish+Awards. The methodology gap between this and a typical 5-phase site SOP is **Art Direction as a named phase** (between Concept and Design) and **Awards/Polish as a named final phase**.
2. **Three "secret-sauce" stages worth building skills for**: (a) **Mood Board / Art Direction** as a deliverable phase, (b) **Concept Prototyping** that uses 3D/runtime tools (Houdini, C4D, vvvv) before the brief is signed, (c) **Polish** as a budgeted ≥30% slice (AT's 80% is extreme; 30–40% is the credible top-tier ratio).
3. **Vocabulary upgrade map** for skill names: rename "design" → "art direction"; rename "implementation" → "designing with code" / "production"; rename "QA" → "polish"; add "concept" + "narrative" + "motion language" as first-class deliverables; add a "lab" track that runs in parallel.
4. **Two divergent value-system axes** the skill library should let users pick: concept-first vs strategy-first; build-from-scratch vs system-extensibility.
5. **Methodology-as-self-rebrand**: every top agency uses its own site/rebrand as the proving ground — the skill library should include a "self-rebrand / portfolio-as-methodology" track.
